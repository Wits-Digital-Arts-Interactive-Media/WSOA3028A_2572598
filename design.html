Inspirational Websites

A lot of the webpage ideas I browsed through were from current artists that I enjoy and I looked at the set up of their page to gain an idea of what mine could look like based of gaining inspiration from some elements of the webpage.

  • This example from June Digan is one that I enjoy a lot. The page seems very filled but not clustered, its placement of images and how they interact with the user make it seem very vibrant. I want the user to interact with the site and feel the vibrancy of my work.

Reference: Junedigann.com

Another example which I enjoyed was this website by Jessica Maxwell Bearden. Again, a very simple website that has the image layout in a way that doesn't draw too much away but rather draws you to exploring the site. Not overwhelming and it seems very easy to interact with.

Interface Elements

Home Page

  • The home page will have a background picture like the one used in the example above. There will be a navigation panel at the top with the rest of the pages as buttons.
  • There will be an information section about the website as you scroll down.
  • Although I want my profile page to be the page will my socials, I am thinking of including my socials at the very bottom of the page.

Portfolio

  • This page will contain my portfolio. As the user scrolls down, they can see more sections/folders of different portfolio types.
  • If they click on the various portfolio types it will open up in more detail where they scroll down.
  • I want it laid out in a minimalistic manner, there probably wont be a background image for this.

Profile

  • This will be the section of myself where the user can see my story about myself, my life, my history and most importantly my C.V.
  • The first section will be a picture about me with the story about myself. Then as the user scrolls down there will be more images with various activities that relate to my C.V.
  • They can also click onto different sections of my profile if they wish to read more.

Design

  • This will be the page where the user can scroll and see every design process about the page and my creative process that went through for this page.
  • Like the other page, users can click on each design to view the full design in detail.

Essays

  • his page, the user can scroll down and see the various essays. It will split into minimized section where the user clicks on each essay to expand.

Blogs

  • The blog will be split in sections of their date where the user can see a sneak peak of each one with a picture where they will click on the blog they want to read.

Style Guide

This is just a rough idea which I will expand but:

  • I wish to have a very simple and minimalistic font, maybe Calibri. I will be deciding on the font still but the colour will be decided based on the background. I want a nude brown or cream white background. For the page buttons, they will be a bit darker type of brown to contrast with the background.
  • The home page will mostly have an image as a background.
