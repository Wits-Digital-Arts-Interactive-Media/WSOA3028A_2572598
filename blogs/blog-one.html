Week 3: Module 3

Interaction Design

Interaction Design: Before I get into the details of the slides, I would first like to explain what I think interaction is and what relation it may have to the web. Interaction for me is a form of communication or exchange of information from one platform or person to another. It means that in the form of the web, it involves engagement between people and technology. It can also include communication between individuals in digital spaces like social media. Interaction is important for user experiences and collaboration in different situations.

When I think of interaction within web design, I think of creating a website that has a layout that compliments its interface. The Interaction Design slideshows discusses how concepts of interaction design and interface design are interlinked. Interaction design focuses on the meeting point between an active body and something that can be affected, such as a user interacting with a website or a game. When I read further, it discusses topics that we learnt in FVPA back in first year such as signs, signifiers and the signified which I think all make sense now in the current context of how it can be applied to web design and interaction. When we look at things like certain web icons or texts, that represents the signifier and with that I began to question how can all three of these be set up in a way that it effectively communicates a good representation of interaction. Furthermore, I also began looking into how it involves responsiveness, non-random behaviour, and requiring non-trivial effort from the user. The interface is the symbolic representation of this interaction and enables and embodies the potential interaction. It is a semiotic system, using signification systems to communicate meaning through signs, such as hyperlinks. The article also covers the process of designing an interface, including alignment, user experience, and interface elements such as input controls and navigational components.
